“Christmas in England” is an essay written by Algernon Blackwood, was first published in 1890 in Methodist Magazine, and is believed to be in the public domain. Digital scans can be found at the Google Books. A transcription was produced by Paul King and transcripts can be found at Github.

The work was translated from English to Esperanto as “Kristnasko en Anglio” in 2023 by Paul King and that translation is released under the CC0 1.0 Universal (CC0 1.0) Public Domain Dedication.

The cover page was adapted from The Christmas Anthem by G. P. Jacomb-Hood.

The cover and title pages feature the League Spartan and Sorts Mill Goudy typefaces created in 2014 and 2009 by The League of Moveable Type.
